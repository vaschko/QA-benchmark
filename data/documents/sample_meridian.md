# The Meridian-7 Tidal Power Plant

The Meridian-7 tidal power plant was commissioned in 2017 in the fictional
Bay of Carrowmere. It was the first project of the engineer Lena Hargrove, who
proposed it after the unusually strong spring tides of 2013. The plant sits at
the narrowest point of the bay, where the tidal range reaches 9.4 metres.

At launch, Meridian-7 operated 18 turbines with a combined capacity of 220
megawatts. By 2021 the operator, Carrowmere Energy, had expanded the array to 30
turbines and a capacity of 360 megawatts. The plant supplies roughly 140,000
households and is managed from a control centre in the town of Felston.

The most distinctive feature is the turbine model "Drift-9", which can rotate to
capture both the incoming and the outgoing tide. Each Drift-9 weighs 84 tonnes
and is expected to last 25 years before a full overhaul. In 2019 a faulty seal
flooded turbine number 12, taking it offline for three months and cutting annual
output by an estimated 4 percent.

Environmental monitoring is led by the marine biologist Dr. Idris Okafor, whose
team tracks the local seal population. Surveys show the colony grew from 210
animals in 2017 to 340 in 2022, which Dr. Okafor attributes to reduced boat
traffic around the protected installation. Carrowmere Energy publishes these
findings every year in the openly available "Carrowmere Review".
